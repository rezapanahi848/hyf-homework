Ingredients:
- 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
- 2 onions, finely chopped
- 3 cloves of garlic, minced
- 2 carrots, sliced
- 2 potatoes, diced
- 1 can of diced tomatoes
- 4 cups of chicken broth
- 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
- 1 teaspoon of dried rosemary
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 2 tablespoons of olive oil
- Chopped parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions:
1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
2. Add the chopped onions and minced garlic to the pot and saut√© until they are soft and translucent.
3. Add the chicken pieces to the pot and cook until they are browned on all sides.
4. Pour in the chicken broth and diced tomatoes, and bring the mixture to a boil.
5. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the stew simmer for about 30 minutes.
6. Add the sliced carrots, diced potatoes, dried thyme, dried rosemary, salt, and pepper to the pot.
7. Cover the pot again and let the stew simmer for an additional 20-30 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
8. Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary.
9. Serve the chicken stew hot, garnished with chopped parsley if desired.

Enjoy your homemade chicken stew!
